The Trivium: How We Teach

We teach students how to think by matching instruction to their natural developmental stage. This three-part process is called the Trivium.

While we are launching with the Grammar Stage (K-2), our vision is to grow with your child through all three stages.

The Launch Plan

Fall 2027: Launching Grades K-2

Future: Adding one grade per year until 12th grade.

The Grammar Stage (K–5)

Enrollment Open for K-2

In these formative years, the mind is open and eager. We fill it with wonder and truth through joyful discovery, recitation, and memorization.

Core Subjects:
  • Phonics-based reading
  • Classic children’s literature
  • Chronological history
  • Mathematics focused on mastery
Enrichment:
  • Latin (Language structure)
  • Scripture & Theology
  • Music & Art

The Logic Stage (6–8)

As students begin to question, we equip them to think clearly. Instruction moves from what to why, exploring order and meaning through formal logic and debate.

The Rhetoric Stage (9–12)

In the final stage, knowledge matures into wisdom. Students learn to synthesize ideas and communicate with clarity, conviction, and grace. Seniors defend a thesis and craft a masterpiece of woodworking.

Hands, Heart, & Mind

We believe education must be practical and tangible. We integrate hands-on skills to teach discipline, safety, and quiet focus.

  • Archery: The physical embodiment of our metaphor. Aim, tension, and release.
  • Woodworking: Shaping materials with care and precision, mirroring how God shapes us.
